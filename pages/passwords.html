Passwords

Passwords are used on many websites to help protect sensitive information, thats why its important to have strong passwords. An upwards of 1,000,000 passwords are stolen each week. Common passwords such as 123456, password, or any word from the dictionary are easy to guess. Adding special characters, random capitalization, and numbers to your password makes it significantly harder to guess. For a 10 digit password using only lowercase letters there are 1e+26 options but if you take that same 10 digit password and use uppercase and lowercase letters along with numbers and symbols you get 1e+102 combinations, a password that has over a googol different combinations, now imagine how strong your password would be if it had 100 of those characters. Below are some general rules for keeping accounts safe.



Make sure to use a long password
Use a mixture of upper and lowercase letters
Use numbers and symbols
Change your password every few months
If your account has been acessed by someone other than you, change your password immediately
Don't share your passwords with anybody
Don't reuse your passwords
Don't make your password easy to guess (Simple words)
Use a password manager
